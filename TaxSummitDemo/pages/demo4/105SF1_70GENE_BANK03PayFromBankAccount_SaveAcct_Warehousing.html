Instructions

Bank routing number

Enter your bank's routing number as it appears on your check. The routing number is the nine-digit number that identifies your bank and always starts with 0, 1, 2, or 3. Don't enter the account number or check number as part of the routing number. If you have any questions about this number, please contact your bank or financial institution.

Bank account number

Enter your account number as it appears on your check. The account number is generally to the right of the routing number. Don't enter the routing number or check number as part of the account number. If you have any questions about this number, please contact your bank or financial institution.

Account type

Select the account type from which you want the funds withdrawn.

Account holder (John Doe or XYZ Corp.)

Enter the name(s) of the account holder(s) from which the amount will be withdrawn. For example, enter John and Mary Smith if that is the name on the account.

Save account

Check this box if you want the bank account information saved for display back to you in the future. This will allow you to use the bank account information for future payments without having to re-enter it each time you make a payment.

Account description

Enter a bank account description. If you save this account and use it to make future payments, the system will display the description to you. (Example: sales tax bank account)

Payment date

This is the date your payment will be considered received by the Department. We will only take the amount of money that you authorize and we will not debit your account before the specified date. Your payment date cannot exceed the due date and cannot be made in the past. If your payment date falls on a weekend or holiday, the payment will be withdrawn from your account on the next business day.
